REPORTER: "Profiling with Ctrl-C" refers to a surprisingly simple yet effective technique for understanding where your software is spending its time, Arjav. The basic idea is to repeatedly hit Ctrl-C at random intervals and then examine the program's stack trace each time it pauses. By collecting enough of these stack traces, you can identify which functions or lines of code are most frequently appearing at the top of the stack, indicating where the program is spending the most execution time. It's a form of statistical profiling, but without the overhead of complex profiling tools. Its usefulness lies in its low overhead, ease of implementation, and ability to quickly pinpoint performance bottlenecks in a running application, making it a quick diagnostic for developers.
